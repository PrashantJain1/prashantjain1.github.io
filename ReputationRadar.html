Strategy and Scope

ReputationRadar is a mobile app concept for SMEs that aggregates fragmented public reputation signals, converts them into a simple Reputation Score, and highlights urgent alerts with AI-generated summaries and remedy suggestions.

Problem statement

SMEs often discover reputation problems too late because reviews, forum discussions and media mentions are split across multiple platforms. Manual monitoring is time-consuming and inconsistent.

Value proposition

The app gives SME teams a single mobile-first monitoring space with scans, alerts, analytics and lightweight billing that matches visible feature value.

MVP scope

Authentication, business verification, source connection, Reputation Score, manual scans, alert management, Analytics Dashboard, settings, subscription plans and billing.

Out of scope

No automated posting, no team collaboration workflows, and no competitor benchmarking. This keeps the concept realistic and safe in ambition.

Interview Notes

We collected four interviews by talking to people in-depth, and summarised their pain points and motivation for such an app.

INT-01

Nina Krause · Owner, 14-person home accessories webshop

Context: Checks Google Reviews and Trustpilot manually every few days and only notices Reddit or news mentions when a customer forwards them.

Quote: “I can usually tell when something is going wrong, but I notice it too late. By the time I read the comments, the post has already spread.”

Key need: Needs one place to see risk early without reading every platform separately.

INT-02

Daniel Weber · Marketing Manager, local food delivery SME

Context: Tracks campaign results in spreadsheets and screenshots review spikes after promotions. Wants a trend view rather than isolated comments.

Quote: “The issue is not finding one bad review. The issue is seeing whether reputation is improving or sliding over time.”

Key need: Needs trend analytics and a simple score that can be explained to leadership.

INT-03

Fatima Yildiz · Customer Support Lead, repair-services SME

Context: Looks for negative feedback to prepare responses, but manual monitoring takes too long and complaints are not prioritised well.

Quote: “My team can respond quickly, but only if we know which complaint matters first.”

Key need: Needs alert prioritisation, summaries, and concrete remedy suggestions.

INT-04

Jonas Hartmann · Co-founder, growing SME software reseller

Context: Budget-sensitive and cautious about paying for software that feels vague or enterprise-heavy.

Quote: “If we pay monthly, I need to understand exactly what extra value we get.”

Key need: Needs a simple subscription structure tied to visible feature value.

Key Insights

Insight 1. Users value early warning more than exhaustive raw data collection.
Insight 2. A single Reputation Score is useful only when users can drill down into the underlying alerts.
Insight 3. Trend visibility is necessary for understanding whether the brand is recovering or declining over time.
Insight 4. Alert handling must prioritise severity and suggest the next response step.
Insight 5. SMEs need a transparent subscription structure that matches visible feature value.

System Architecture

Client layerAndroid / mobile client shows onboarding, scans, alerts, analytics, settings and billing screens.
Integration layerConnectors ingest Google Reviews, Trustpilot, NewsAPI and Reddit into raw source records.
Processing layerNLP services classify sentiment, compose AI Threat Summaries and support remedy suggestions.
Core servicesAuth, verification, score engine, alert service, notification service and billing service coordinate state changes.

AI and Big Data Rationale

Big Data Advantages

ReputationRadar combines multiple external data types and volumes over time: reviews, forum posts, comments and media mentions. The concept addresses variety, ongoing inflow and historical accumulation.

AI-powered application

AI is applied for sentiment classification, alert summarisation and remedy suggestion generation. The app does not claim fully autonomous decision-making, and only acts as an aid to the SMEs.

Scope and cost

The scope includes scheduled scans and summary generation only where value is clear. This keeps model usage and API cost more realistic for SMEs than a constant real-time monitoring claim.

Alignment

The business value is in terms of avoidance of disasters, the data pipeline is scalable to multiple information sources, the architecture has enough depth to extract meaningful insights, and the billing model shows cost awareness.

Feasibility Analysis

Technical

Feasible with common mobile, backend, queue, database and payment technologies. The hardest parts are connector reliability and good AI design. The challenges are SME-aligned truth and language-sentiment detection.

Operational

Safe because the app supports decision-making rather than automating public responses. SME teams remain in control.

Financial

Subscription tiers create a clear revenue model. Analytics is placed behind higher-value plans to justify the paid tier.

Legal / privacy

Company verification, login security, billing records and user notification preferences should be handled under normal GDPR and payment-provider controls.
